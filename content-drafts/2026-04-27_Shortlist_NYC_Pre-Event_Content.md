## Variant A — "The Yext-to-Windmill arc"

**Thread:** The most pedigreed founder in the room is going lean by choice. That tells you how smart-money ex-founders are reading 2026.

```
Tonight at Shortlist NYC #4: six founders pitching what they're building, who they're hiring. The most pedigreed person in the room is building unfunded.

Brian Distelburger took Yext public on the NYSE in 2017. Tonight he's at Betaworks pitching Windmill — the AI Management Company, deliberately unfunded in 2026.

The capital-stage spread of the rest of the lineup is the curation:
→ Adonis: $40M Series C last month (4x revenue 2025, 130% NRR)
→ GIC: $8.7M USV-led seed in December
→ Sparrow, Rediem, Zo Computer — at three other distinct stages

One room, five hiring postures. The most experienced founder is the one going lean.

That tells you how smart-money ex-founders are reading 2026 — not "raise big and hire fast" but "build small with people you've worked with before." The AI talent layer being assembled in NYC is dense at every stage *except* the one most operators chase.

If you're an operator weighing fund-it vs. join-it: which signal carries more weight for you right now — the unfunded ex-Yext bet, or the Series-C-with-130%-NRR rocket?

#NYCAI #FoundingGTM #EnterpriseAI #Shortlist
```

**Quality check:**
- Hook: specific number (2017 IPO) + inversion (most pedigreed = most lean). Stops the scroll.
- 3 specific data points (Yext IPO, Adonis $40M / 4x / 130% NRR, GIC $8.7M).
- Insight passes the expert-pause test: the unfunded-ex-Yext-bet-as-signal is non-obvious.
- Documentarian voice: reporter (specific facts) + analyst (interpretation).
- No anti-pattern phrases. No "I'm excited." No "leverage / unlock / ecosystem."
- CTA is specific and answerable (one of two structured options).
- 4 hashtags, topic-specific.

## Variant B — "AI isn't the differentiator anymore. Workflow collapse is."

**Thread:** Six founders pitched at Shortlist; not one led with AI. Every single one led with which workflow they're collapsing. The differentiator has shifted.

```
Tonight's Shortlist NYC #4: six founders, six pitches. Not one of them led with AI.

Every single founder pitched what *workflow* they're collapsing.

→ Sparrow: 6 weeks of credit-union onboarding compressed to 6 minutes
→ Adonis: manual healthcare claims work handed to AI orchestration ($95M+ raised since 2022)
→ GIC: an entire company collapsed into one founder + agent swarm (USV-led seed Dec 2025)
→ Windmill: blank-page performance reviews replaced by data already in your work tools

A year ago, "AI" was the headline. Tonight, it's table stakes — and the differentiator is which workflow you can fully collapse.

The buyer story changes when the pitch changes. "AI-powered" stops working. "We replaced the 6-week thing with a 6-minute thing" works.

Operators in transition — what's the workflow you'd most want collapsed in your last role, and who's building toward it?

#NYCAI #EnterpriseAI #Workflow #Shortlist
```

**Quality check:**
- Hook: specific observation that inverts the dominant narrative (AI is no longer the headline).
- 4 concrete examples (Sparrow, Adonis, GIC, Windmill) — slightly above the 2-3 ideal but each is a single-line, scannable.
- Insight: "the differentiator has shifted from AI to workflow collapse" — sharp, contrarian, supported by the room.
- Documentarian voice: reporter (specific pitches) + analyst (the meta-shift).
- No anti-patterns.
- CTA is specific (asks for the reader's own workflow + a candidate company).
- 4 hashtags.
